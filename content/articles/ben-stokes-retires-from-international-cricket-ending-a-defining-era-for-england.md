Ben Stokes, the talismanic all-rounder who carried English cricket through its most decorated decade, announced his retirement from international cricket on Sunday, bringing the curtain down on a career that redefined what the format could be.



The 34-year-old, who captained England's Test side from 2022, cited the cumulative toll of chronic knee injuries and a desire to "be present" for his family and franchise commitments as the reasons behind the decision.

"I have given this team everything for the last decade and a half. There comes a moment when the body, and the family, ask for the rest of you back. That moment has come."

Ben Stokes, in a statement issued through the ECB*

**A career defined by impossible moments:**

Stokes leaves the international game as one of the most decorated all-rounders England has ever produced:

\- \*\*6,500+ Test runs\*\* at an average just under 36, with 13 centuries

\- \*\*204 Test wickets\*\* at 32, including six five-wicket hauls

\- \*\*Architect of the 2019 World Cup final\*\* — the unbeaten 84 against New Zealand that forced the Super Over

\- \*\*Headingley, 2019\*\* — the 135 not out against Australia that \*Wisden\* later called the greatest Test innings of the modern era

\- \*\*2022 T20 World Cup final\*\*  the calm 52 not out against Pakistan that sealed England's second white-ball world title

**The Bazball captain:**

His elevation to the Test captaincy in April 2022, alongside head coach Brendon McCullum, ushered in the era now known as "Bazball" a deliberately aggressive, risk-embracing approach that reshaped England's red-ball cricket and, with it, the way Test cricket is talked about globally.

Under Stokes's leadership, England won \*\*22 of his 36 Tests in charge\*\*, including memorable series wins in Pakistan and India, and forced draws into thrilling final sessions.

**Reactions pour in:**

Tributes flowed within minutes of the announcement. Pakistan captain Babar Azam called Stokes "the bravest cricketer of our generation, a man who turned losing positions into wins by sheer will." Indian batter Virat Kohli posted simply: \*"What a player. What a man. Thank you, Stokesy."\*

ECB chair told reporters: "He is the most influential English cricketer since Ian Botham, and arguably the greatest match-winner of any era. We will not see his like again soon."

**What's next:**

Stokes will continue playing in franchise leagues including the IPL, where he is contracted to Chennai Super Kings, and \*\*The Hundred\*\* for Northern Superchargers. He has also signalled an interest in moving into coaching once his playing days are fully behind him.

England's next assignment is a five-Test home series against Australia beginning in November. The selectors will name a new Test captain within the next fortnight, with Joe Root, Harry Brook, and Ollie Pope considered the leading contenders.
